# The Gardener of Zero-Days

> An original poem written for the SecureBananaLabs bug-bounty community.

---

## I. The Surface

We browse the orchard, skins unbroken,  
glossy and ripe with promised function—  
no worm tunnels the perfect curve,  
no rot beneath the painted verve.  
The user trusts. The user bites.  
The user sleeps through quiet nights  
while somewhere in the compost heap  
a seed of chaos starts to creep.

---

## II. The Root System

Beneath the soil, logic forks  
like mycelium in ancient parks:  
an `if` that never asked its `else`,  
a `try` that swallowed its own pulse,  
a promise hanging in the air  
unresolved, a silent prayer  
to servers humming in the dark,  
where packets leave their phantom mark.  
The gardener kneels. The gardener sees.  
The gardener knows what rot can be  
in silicon and syntax trees.

---

## III. The Hunt

She moves with patience, not with haste,  
through JSON forests, API waste,  
where rate limits thrum like fences  
and headers leak their hidden senses.  
Each `200 OK` a mask she lifts,  
each redirect a cliff she shifts,  
until the boundary blurs and bends—  
where client ends and server ends,  
where trust was placed and trust was spent,  
where validation came and went  
like summer rain on porous stone,  
like passwords hashed but not alone.

---

## IV. The Disclosure

There is no violence in her hands,  
no greed that drives her through these lands.  
She maps the fault with surgeon's grace,  
exposes root but leaves the face  
unbruised, untorn, intact, and whole—  
a gift more rare than any gold:  
the knowledge that the system lives,  
the knowledge that the system gives  
itself again to those who ask  
with honest eyes and careful task.  
She pulls the weed. She mends the wall.  
She does not burn the orchard. All  
that ripens after is her praise—  
anonymous, in quiet ways.

---

## V. The Harvest

The bounty paid is never quite  
the measure of the gardener's light.  
What value has a zero-day  
when held in trust, when given away?  
The real reward is harder won:  
the code that runs when morning comes,  
the cart that checks out clean and true,  
the child whose data sleeps on through  
another night of undisturbed  
and ordinary, unobserved  
existence in a world that spins  
on honest work and hidden wins.  
So here she stands, in plain attire,  
between the flame and the firewall,  
tending the garden, line by line,  
harvesting shadows, making them shine.
